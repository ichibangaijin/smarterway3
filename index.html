Read about me. Tell me about you.

A few years ago I set out to teach myself JavaScript by reading programming books. It was such a struggle that I decided I must have lost some learning ability over the years. Then it hit me...

I wasn't a bad learner. The books were bad teachers!
I fought my way through a dozen books, and by brute effort, learned Javascript. But I had to design exercises for myself. Without practice, I couldn't retain anything.

Javascript, I learned, isn't that hard. The books make it hard. So I wrote a book that makes JavaScript easy. And, since exercises are the only way to make the knowledge stick, I programmed 1,750 of them for you.
